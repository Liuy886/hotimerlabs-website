# Web3 Consulting Website Product Plan

## 1. Product Positioning

Build a professional business website for a Web3 consulting company serving exchanges, protocols, token projects, and ecosystem funds. The website should establish trust quickly, explain service capabilities clearly, and convert qualified leads into private consultation calls.

Core positioning:
- A full-cycle Web3 growth and capital-market services partner.
- Strong in project incubation, go-to-market, exchange listing, market making coordination, community growth, and strategic advisory.
- Suitable for early-stage projects, mature token projects, exchanges seeking ecosystem expansion, and institutions entering Web3.

Suggested Chinese positioning line:
> Web3 项目的增长、上市与市场流动性战略伙伴

Suggested English positioning line:
> Strategic Growth, Listing and Liquidity Partner for Web3 Projects

## 2. Target Customers

Primary audiences:
- Web3 project founders and core teams
- Token projects preparing for listing or market expansion
- Exchanges seeking high-quality project resources and market campaigns
- VC-backed crypto startups needing Asian or global market access
- Traditional companies exploring Web3 transformation

Key customer concerns:
- Can you bring real exchange, KOL, community, and liquidity resources?
- Can you design a credible roadmap from product to market?
- Can you avoid low-quality traffic and short-term noise?
- Can you support confidential, high-stakes listing and MM work?
- Do you understand both project-side and exchange-side business logic?

## 3. Website Goals

Business goals:
- Generate qualified consultation leads.
- Present service scope in a clear and premium way.
- Build credibility before direct sales conversations.
- Package complex Web3 services into understandable offerings.
- Support both Chinese and international visitors in later versions.

Conversion goals:
- Primary CTA: Book a Consultation / 预约咨询
- Secondary CTA: Contact via Telegram / Email / WhatsApp
- Tertiary CTA: Download company profile or submit project information

## 4. Recommended Site Structure

Version 1 should be a focused single-page website with strong section navigation. This keeps development fast and message control tight.

Sections:
1. Hero
   - Clear positioning
   - One strong value proposition
   - Primary CTA
   - Trust cues such as service categories or network coverage

2. Who We Serve
   - Project teams
   - Exchanges
   - Funds and ecosystems
   - Traditional enterprises entering Web3

3. Core Services
   - Incubation and Strategy
   - Market Expansion and Campaigns
   - Listing Advisory
   - Market Making Coordination
   - Community and KOL Growth
   - Exchange and Ecosystem Partnerships

4. Engagement Model
   - Diagnosis
   - Strategy design
   - Resource matching
   - Execution management
   - Review and optimization

5. Why Us
   - Web3 native experience
   - Exchange and project-side understanding
   - Integrated growth, listing, and liquidity perspective
   - Confidential execution
   - Global and Asian market access

6. Case / Track Record Placeholder
   - In V1, use anonymized proof points if public logos cannot be shown.
   - Examples: "Supported listing preparation for multiple token projects", "Built regional GTM campaigns", "Coordinated KOL and community growth."

7. Contact / Lead Form
   - Project name
   - Website or deck link
   - Current stage
   - Required services
   - Telegram or email
   - Message

## 5. Service Packaging

Recommended public-facing service names:

### Project Incubation
Positioning, token narrative, roadmap planning, ecosystem strategy, fundraising support, and resource planning.

### Go-to-Market
Market entry strategy, community growth, KOL campaigns, PR planning, ambassador systems, and regional expansion.

### Listing Advisory
Exchange matching, listing readiness review, application material preparation, market timing, and communication support.

### Market Making Coordination
Liquidity strategy, MM partner matching, post-listing performance review, and market structure advisory.

### Exchange Growth Support
Project sourcing, campaign planning, regional partnerships, ecosystem expansion, and user acquisition programs.

## 6. Brand Tone

The website should feel:
- Premium but not flashy
- International but practical
- Trustworthy, discreet, and resource-driven
- Web3-native without excessive jargon
- More like a boutique strategic advisory firm than a loud agency

Visual direction:
- Dark professional base with high-contrast text
- Controlled use of metallic, cyan, or green accent colors
- Data-grid / market-structure visual language
- Avoid meme-heavy, cartoonish, or overly speculative styling

## 8. MVP Scope

V1 deliverables:
- Responsive single-page website
- Chinese copy first
- English-ready structure
- Contact form UI
- Telegram / Email CTA
- Basic SEO metadata
- Mobile-friendly layout
- Privacy-friendly lead collection wording

Out of scope for V1:
- Blog system
- Client portal
- CMS
- Complex animations
- Public case studies requiring legal approval
- Payment or account system

## 9. Execution Plan

Phase 1: Product and Content
- Confirm company name, logo status, and contact channels.
- Finalize service names and homepage copy.
- Decide whether to show public partner/client logos or use anonymous proof points.

Phase 2: Design and Frontend
- Build a polished responsive landing page.
- Create service cards, engagement process, proof points, and lead form.
- Add strong first-screen visual identity.

Phase 3: Conversion and Trust
- Add form validation.
- Add Telegram/email direct contact.
- Add downloadable company profile if needed.
- Add analytics after deployment.

Phase 4: Launch
- Deploy to Vercel, Cloudflare Pages, or similar.
- Connect domain.
- Configure SEO title, description, Open Graph image, and favicon.

## 10. Information Needed From Founder

Minimum required before final copy:
- Company name
- Preferred language: Chinese only, English only, or bilingual
- Public contact method: Telegram, email, WhatsApp, form, or Calendly
- Whether there are public clients, partners, or anonymized achievements
- Geographic focus: Asia, global, MENA, Europe, or specific markets
- Brand preference: conservative institution style or sharper Web3-native style

Recommended default if not yet decided:
- Use a temporary brand name and build a flexible structure.
- Launch in Chinese first.
- Keep partner/client claims anonymized.
- Use Telegram and email as primary lead channels.
